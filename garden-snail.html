Garden Snail

The garden snail, or Cornu aspersum or Cryptomphalus aspersus, is one of the most common and widely known varieties of land snail. It originated in the Mediterranean region, and its range of natural habitats include regions throughout Africa, Asia, and Europe. In addition, it has been introduced to the Americas, Australia, and New Zealand. Garden snails primarily have herbivorous diets, but they can also feed on worms and other snails. Like other snails, they move through the use of a body part known as a foot, and the mucus they secrete helps them do this.
