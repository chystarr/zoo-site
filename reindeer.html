Reindeer

The reindeer, or Rangifer tarandus, is found in regions located far to the north, in North America, Siberia, and Northern Europe. In North America, it is also known as the caribou. Different subspecies of reindeer take both forests and tundras as their habitats. Unlike other species of deer, both male and female reindeer grow antlers. Reindeer are capable of perceiving shorter wavelengths of light than humans can, and as such can see in the ultraviolet range. While reindeer have mainly herbivorous diets, they have been known to sometimes eat bird eggs and rodents.
